# Polish market validation runbook

This runbook prepares the repository side of Issue #22 without claiming that real outreach or validation has happened.

## Start gate

Do not start the seven-day clock until all of the following are true:

- the Polish site is live;
- a real contact destination is confirmed and visible;
- the request flow has a usable copy/manual-send fallback;
- the first three product specifications and test prices are available;
- the owner has selected the outreach start date.

The current unverified `hello@vitao.studio` placeholder must not be used as an active destination.

## Experiment target

During seven consecutive days:

- send 20–30 personal Polish messages;
- publish 1–3 Polish marketplace or social listings;
- use only product facts and qualified price assumptions already present in the repository;
- reply manually to serious inquiries;
- do not use paid advertising;
- do not invent pickup, dimensions, production time, durability, demand, or customer feedback.

## Daily checkpoint

At the end of each day record:

- outreach actions completed versus target;
- relevant visitors or conversations, when measurable;
- product mentions by product;
- conversations about price, size, lead time, or ordering;
- serious quote requests;
- repeated objections or request-flow friction;
- changes made to outreach wording, with the reason.

Do not change the website during the experiment unless a confirmed blocker prevents inquiries. Record the blocker first.

### Recommendation

Choose exactly one:

- **GO** — continue with the leading product and the smallest evidence-backed improvements;
- **PIVOT** — change product, positioning, price hypothesis, or channel based on observed feedback;
- **STOP** — pause further implementation because the experiment did not produce sufficient signal.

Include:

- evidence supporting the decision;
- the leading product or category, if any;
- assumptions that remain unverified;
- the next decision date;
- follow-up issues derived only from observed findings.

## Success-signal reference

Issue #22 defines the initial signals as:

- 20–50 relevant visitors;
- at least five specific product mentions;
- at least two conversations about price, size, lead time, or ordering;
- at least one serious quote request;
- one product or category clearly leading.

These are experiment signals, not fabricated acceptance results. Leave values at zero or blank until real evidence exists.
